The Knave of Hearts

The Queen of Hearts

She made some tarts

All on a summer's day

The Knave of Hearts

He stole those tarts

And took them quite away

The King of Hearts

Called for those tarts

And beat the Knave full sore

The Knave of Hearts

Brought back those tarts

And vowed he'd sin no more

Introduction

Portland High School submitted "The Knave of Hearts" as their play for the 2025 One Act Festival, with director Bri Auspland and assistant director Sawyer Surkin. The judges awarded Lady Violetta with All-Star Cast and Bri Auspland as Judges' Commendation for directing and costuming.

Background

"The Knave of Hearts" by Louise Saunders (1925) is a one-act play based on the poem "The Queen of Hearts" (1782). The poem details an impish Knave who steals the Queen's tarts, only to be brought to justice with a beating. Saunders took this poem and flipped the narrative.

The story takes place in the Kingdom of Hearts where Lady Violetta is to be married to the King of Hearts: Pompdebile. But, as is tradition, she must prove herself worthy of the throne by presenting a dish of her choice to Blue Hose and Yellow Hose, the best bakers in the land. The twist comes when it is revealed Violetta cannot bake at all, leading to her requesting the help of the Knave, where they devise a plot to fool the court. The Knave takes Violetta's tarts and replaces them with tarts made by his wife. When caught, he takes the blame, and the "Queen of Hearts" poem is recited as the events of what happened, therefore covering up Violetta's involvement.
